short answer: NO!, It is not safe for a human to wear depleted uranium armor.
reasons: Hazard /	Explanation
1 - External radiation exposure:	Unshielded DU produces contact dose rates up to 2 mSv/h. That's significant for prolonged wear.
2 - Inhalation hazard:	If the armor is damaged (hit by bullet, shrapnel, or even just scratched), DU dust becomes airborne. Inhaled DU particles lodge in lungs and irradiate internal tissues.
3 - Ingestion hazard:	DU dust can contaminate skin, clothing, and be swallowed.
4 - Kidney toxicity:	DU is a heavy metal that damages kidney function.
5 - Thermal hazard:	DU ignites when abraded or fractured, reaching temperatures over 600°C. If your armor gets hit, it could catch fire on your body.
6 - Carcinogenic concerns:	While debated, studies suggest potential leukemia risk from chronic exposure.

Tungsten gives you the same density without the radiation, toxicity, or fire risk.
